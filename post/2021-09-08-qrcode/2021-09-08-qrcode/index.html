Creating a contact info QR code with LaTeX

September 8, 2021
LaTeX

QR Codes

QR Codes are two dimensional “Quick Response Codes” presented first in the year 1994 by a japanese company. Nowadays QR Codes are widely used and a very effective way to transport short informations.

LaTeX offers a package called pst-barcode which can be used to generate several different barcodes. Here i tried to create a Vcard. Unfortunately the Vcard convention is somewhat strange in the behaviour. So sometimes android phones stick with that generated qr code.

LaTeX template

The LaTeX template may be defined as follows:

\documentclass{standalone}

\usepackage[utf8]{inputenc} 
\usepackage[german]{babel}
\usepackage{pst-barcode}

{\obeylines\gdef^^M{^^J}%
 \catcode`\@=0 %
 \catcode`\\=12 %
 @gdef@n{\n}}

\usepackage{auto-pst-pdf} % uncomment this if used with pdflatex

\begin{document}

\begin{pspicture}(1.08in,1in)
\obeylines
\psbarcode{%
BEGIN:VCARD
VERSION:3.0
N:Saal;Christian;;Dr.;
FN:Dr. Christian Saal
EMAIL:christian.saal@mail.de
TEL:+4915345678109
ORG:Company
TITLE:Research
ADR:;;Street;City;;Postal;Germany
URL:http://www.myurl.de
END:VCARD
}{width=1. height=1.}{qrcode}
\uput{0pt}[90]{90}(2.65,0){\texttt{\tiny{Kontakt}}}
\end{pspicture}

\end{document}

Summary

QR Codes are really usefull in many cases. Check the documentation to get an idea of what you can do with barcodes pst-barcode
